### Project 1: Foundational NLP Pipelines & Preprocessing Engine (Chapters 1 & 2)
* **Focus:** Deep theoretical analysis of text retrieval, comprehensive tokenization paradigms, advanced syntactic/semantic extraction, and downstream ML pipeline evaluation.
* **Theoretical Framework:**
  * *Text Processing & Architecture:* Functional comparison of algorithmic Stemming vs. morphological Lemmatization. Comprehensive architectural evaluation of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) across its three primary phases, exploring its mitigation of hallucinations and audit trails over direct LLM inferencing in enterprise applications.
  * *System Design & Analysis:* Contrasting Extractive vs. Generative Question-Answering (QA) frameworks under precision-critical medical constraints, focusing on traceability and unanswerable queries. Architecting a multi-tier customer support QA pipeline for digital service ecosystems, mapping localized data transformations, and assessing technical and ethical limits.
  * *Linguistic Scaling & Tokenization:* Investigating the "Garbage In, Garbage Out" axiom in textual data preparation. Systematic profiling of Character, Word, and Subword tokenizers (BPE, WordPiece) to analyze vocabulary footprints, Out-of-Vocabulary (OOV) collision rates, and compute costs.
* **Practical Implementation:**
  * *Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):* Programmatic ingestion of real-world social media datasets (Elon Musk's historical tweets corpus) via Pandas to audit null fields, map structural document lengths via histograms, and evaluate user engagement distributions (retweets/favorites) via boxplots and statistical medians.
  * *Deterministic Text Normalization:* Engineering a sequential regular expression (Regex) cleaning engine that handles HTML decoding, standardizes case formats, eliminates short-URL links, preserves critical punctuation tags, and replaces raw numeric variables with a standard `NUM` token while leaving structural units intact.
  * *Feature Extraction & Regex Matching:* Designing complex Regex patterns to cleanly isolate and track domain-specific text segments, including alphanumeric monetary indicators (scales in K, M, T), raw percentages, email addresses, and regional hashtags directly from the uncleaned source layers.
  * *Comparative Tokenization & Compaction:* Conducting a controlled experimental benchmark tracking Whitespace splitting, `nltk.word_tokenize`, and Hugging Face's `BertTokenizer` across randomized subsets, calculating vocabulary density and OOV distributions. Running comparative linguistic compaction routines using NLTK Porter Stemmers vs. spaCy POS-informed Lemmatizers to map volume reductions.
  * *Advanced Syntactic Parsing & NER:* Deploying high-fidelity spaCy pipelines to classify global Part-of-Speech (POS) tags, constructing bar charts of dominant grammatical categories (NOUN, VERB, ADJ, ADV), and computing the Noun-to-Verb style ratio. Running full-dataset Named Entity Recognition (NER) to isolate and frequency-rank real-world entities (PERSON, ORG, GPE, MONEY).
  * *Downstream Model Validation:* Quantifying preprocessing effectiveness by passing the varying text arrays into a supervised, fixed-seed Logistic Regression model tasked with predicting tweet virality (top 25% popularity threshold), establishing direct empirical links between text quality and classification accuracy.
